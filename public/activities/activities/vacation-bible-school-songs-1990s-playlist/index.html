Vacation Bible School Songs from the 1990s: A Nostalgic Playlist for Today’s Kids

Introduction

Remember the energetic beats and simple truths of vacation bible school songs from the 1990s? This beloved playlist continues to shape young hearts today. The 1990s brought us timeless VBS classics that taught biblical principles through catchy melodies and memorable motions. Creating a vacation bible school songs 1990s playlist helps today’s children experience the same joy and spiritual growth that touched a generation. These songs combine fun with fundamental faith lessons, making them perfect for both home worship time and church programs. Whether you’re planning a VBS event or looking to bring more worship into your family’s daily routine, these 90s favorites offer the perfect blend of nostalgia and timeless truth. Parents and teachers can use these proven songs to build a strong spiritual foundation through music.

Scripture

“Let everything that has breath praise the Lord. Praise the Lord!” (Psalm 150:6)

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” (Psalm 98:4)

Worship Ideas

  1. “Our God Is An Awesome God” - Have children raise their arms high during “awesome” and make wave motions for “reigns”
  2. “Lord I Lift Your Name on High” - Teach the traditional hand motions that tell the story of Jesus coming down to earth and rising to heaven
  3. “Every Move I Make” - Create a follow-the-leader circle where kids add their own movements to praise God

FAQs

Q: What age group is best suited for 1990s VBS songs? A: These songs are typically ideal for children ages 4-12, though younger children can enjoy the motions and older kids often appreciate the nostalgia and deeper meanings.

Q: How can I incorporate these songs into our daily family routine? A: Try playing them during morning routines, car rides, or create a special “worship time” before bed. Make it interactive by adding motions or instruments.

Q: Where can I find recordings of classic 1990s VBS songs? A: You can find these songs on streaming platforms like Spotify and YouTube, or purchase compilations from Christian music publishers. Many church resource websites also offer downloadable versions with accompanying motions and lesson plans.
