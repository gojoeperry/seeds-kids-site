Worship Ideas

  1. “Away in a Manger” with Motions
  • “Have children rock imaginary baby Jesus during the first verse”
  • “Make twinkling star motions during “the stars in the sky”
  • “Fold hands in prayer for the final verse”
  1. “Joy to the World” Action Song
  • “Jump on “Joy”
  • “Make a round earth shape with arms on “World”
  • “Create a crown with hands on “King”
  1. “Little Drummer Boy” with Rhythm
  • “Provide small drums or hand motions”
  • “Let children take turns being the drummer boy”

FAQs

What age group are these Christmas Sunday school songs best suited for?

These songs are generally most effective for children ages 3-10, but can be adapted for both younger and older groups. Simpler songs like “Away in a Manger” work well for preschoolers, while older children can handle more complex songs with multiple verses.

How can I teach these songs effectively to wiggly children?

Break the songs into small, manageable sections and always include motions or actions. Start with the chorus, add verses gradually, and make it interactive. Use visual aids like picture cards or puppets to help maintain attention and reinforce the message.

Where can I find more Christmas Sunday school songs and resources?

Many Christian publishing companies offer children’s Christmas music collections. Look for resources from publishers like Group Publishing, LifeWay, or Seeds Family Worship. Online platforms like YouTube also offer Christian children’s channels with seasonal content. Your local Christian bookstore likely has songbooks and CDs specifically designed for children’s ministry.
