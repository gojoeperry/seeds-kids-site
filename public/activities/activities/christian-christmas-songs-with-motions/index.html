Christian Christmas Songs with Motions for Kids

Introduction

Teaching children Christian Christmas songs with motions is a wonderful way to help them celebrate the birth of Jesus while engaging their whole bodies in worship. When kids combine music, movement, and biblical truth, they create lasting memories and develop a deeper understanding of God’s love. Christian Christmas songs with motions allow children to express their joy physically while learning important truths about the nativity story. These interactive worship experiences help young ones stay focused, make learning fun, and create meaningful connections with their faith. Whether you’re a parent, Sunday school teacher, or children’s ministry leader, incorporating motion-based Christmas songs into your holiday celebrations can spark enthusiasm and help kids grow spiritually during this special season.

Worship Ideas

  1. “Joy to the World” with Star Motions
  • “Raise hands high and wiggle fingers for “stars”
  • “Make circular motions for “world”
  • “Point to heaven when singing about the Lord”
  1. “Away in a Manger” with Cradle Rock
  • “Rock arms like holding baby Jesus”
  • “Make animal sounds for the cattle”
  • “Form star shapes with arms for “stars in the sky”
  1. “Silent Night” with Candlelight Actions
  • “Hold up pretend candles”
  • “Sway gently side to side”
  • “Make sleeping motions during “sleep in heavenly peace”

Devotional Thought

Just as the angels sang to announce Jesus’s birth, we too can use our voices and bodies to praise Him. When we sing and move in worship, we’re showing God how happy we are that He sent Jesus to be our Savior. Every motion we make can be a gift of praise to God.

Prayer: Dear Jesus, thank you for coming as a baby on Christmas. Help us to worship you with our whole hearts, voices, and bodies. Amen.

FAQs

Q: What age group is best for learning Christmas songs with motions? A: Children ages 2-8 typically enjoy and benefit most from songs with motions, but these activities can be adapted for any age group. Even toddlers can participate in simple movements, while older children can help lead younger ones.

Q: How can I teach motions to a large group of children? A: Break down the motions into small, simple steps. Demonstrate each movement slowly, then practice one section at a time. Use repetition and make it fun by having children mirror your actions before combining motions with the music.

Q: Where can I find Christian Christmas songs with motions? A: Many children’s ministry websites offer free resources, including YouTube channels dedicated to kids’ worship. Popular sources include Seeds Family Worship, Go Fish Kids, and Yancy Ministries. Your local Christian bookstore may also carry DVDs and resources specifically designed for motion-based worship.
