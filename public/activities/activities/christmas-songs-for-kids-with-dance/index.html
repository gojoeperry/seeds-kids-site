Christmas Songs for Kids with Dance: Joyful Worship for Young Hearts

Introduction

Christmas songs for kids with dance provide an exciting and meaningful way for children to celebrate the birth of Jesus while engaging their whole bodies in worship. When children combine music, movement, and the Christmas message, they create lasting memories and deeper spiritual connections. These interactive worship experiences help young ones understand the joy of Christ’s birth through age-appropriate, fun activities that capture their attention and energy.

As parents and teachers, incorporating Christmas songs for kids with dance into your holiday celebrations can transform ordinary moments into sacred spaces of praise. This active form of worship allows children to express their love for Jesus naturally and enthusiastically, helping them build a foundation of faith that goes beyond just sitting quietly in church.

Scripture

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

“Let them praise His name with dancing; let them sing praises to Him with tambourine and harp.” - Psalm 149:3

Worship Ideas

  1. “Joy to the World” with Star Jumps
  • “Jump up with arms raised like stars on “Joy”
  • “Spin in circles during “the world”
  • “Make wave motions for “let earth receive her King”
  1. “Away in a Manger” with Gentle Motions
  • “Rock baby Jesus during verses”
  • “Make twinkling star hands for “the stars in the sky”
  • “Fold hands in prayer for final verse”
  1. “Little Drummer Boy” with Rhythm Movements
  • “March in place to the beat”
  • “Pat-a-rum-pum-pum with hand motions”
  • “Take turns being the “drummer”

Devotional Thought

Just as the angels danced and sang to celebrate Jesus’s birth, we too can praise God with our whole bodies. When we dance and sing for Jesus, we’re showing Him how happy we are that He came to earth as our Savior. Every jump, twirl, and motion can be a gift of praise to God.

Prayer: Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us music and dance to praise you. Help us share your joy with others through our songs and movements. Amen.

FAQs

What age group is best for Christmas dance worship?

Christmas songs with dance movements are ideal for children ages 2-12, but can be adapted for any age. Toddlers enjoy simple motions, while older children can handle more complex choreography. The key is matching the movements to your group’s abilities.

How can I incorporate dance worship at home during Christmas?

Start by choosing familiar Christmas songs and adding simple movements. Create a daily worship time after dinner, or make it part of your morning routine. Use everyday moments like decorating the tree or baking cookies as opportunities to sing and dance in praise.

Where can I find more Christmas worship dance resources?

Look for Christian children’s ministry websites, YouTube channels dedicated to kids’ worship, or your local Christian bookstore. Many churches also offer seasonal workshops or materials for children’s Christmas programs that include dance elements.
