Christmas Songs for Kids: Jingle Bells

Introduction

Teaching children Christmas songs for kids like “Jingle Bells” can be a wonderful gateway to deeper spiritual conversations about the joy of the Christmas season. While “Jingle Bells” isn’t explicitly a religious song, its cheerful melody and easy-to-learn lyrics make it perfect for transitioning into more Christ-centered worship moments with children. When we combine fun Christmas songs for kids like “Jingle Bells” with biblical teaching, we create memorable experiences that help children associate the holiday season with both joy and Jesus’s birth. The familiar rhythm and repetitive nature of the song helps children engage confidently in group worship, building their comfort with expressing praise through music.

Scripture

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

“Let them praise his name with dancing and make music to him with timbrel and harp.” - Psalm 149:3

Worship Ideas

  • “Create a “Jingle Bells Praise Band” using real bells, shakers, or homemade instruments”
  • “Teach children to sing one verse of “Jingle Bells” followed by “Jesus Loves Me” to connect secular and sacred songs”
  • “Add Christian-themed verses to the familiar tune:” “Jesus came, Jesus came, born on Christmas Day! In a manger far away, our Savior came to stay, hey!”

Devotional Thought

Just as bells ring out to announce something special, Jesus’s birth was announced by angels singing joyfully in the sky. When we sing happy songs like “Jingle Bells,” we can remember how much joy Jesus brings to our lives. Every time we hear bells ringing during Christmas, let’s think about how God sent His Son to bring joy to the whole world.

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us music and joy. Help us remember You are the reason we celebrate. Amen.”

FAQs

What age group is best for learning “Jingle Bells” in a worship context?

“Jingle Bells” is ideal for children ages 2-8, though older children can enjoy more complex arrangements. The simple melody and repetitive lyrics make it especially suitable for preschoolers and early elementary students.

How can I make “Jingle Bells” more Christ-centered for my children?

Connect the song to Jesus by discussing how the joy we feel singing it reflects the joy of Christ’s birth. Create new verses about Jesus, use it as a transition to Christian Christmas carols, or incorporate it into Bible lessons about celebrating God’s gifts.

Where can I find Christian versions or arrangements of “Jingle Bells”?

Many Christian children’s ministry websites offer free downloadable sheet music and lyrics for faith-based “Jingle Bells” variations. Christian bookstores often carry children’s Christmas music collections, and platforms like YouTube have numerous Christian children’s versions available.
