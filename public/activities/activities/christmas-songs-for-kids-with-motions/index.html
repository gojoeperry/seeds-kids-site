Christmas Songs for Kids with Motions: Making Holiday Worship Fun

Introduction

Christmas songs for kids with motions offer a wonderful way to help children connect with the true meaning of Christmas through active worship. When children combine music with movement, they engage multiple senses, making the learning experience more meaningful and memorable. These interactive Christmas songs for kids with motions not only make worship fun but also help young ones internalize biblical truths about Jesus’s birth. As parents and teachers, we can use these engaging worship experiences to plant seeds of faith that will grow throughout their lives. The combination of familiar holiday tunes with simple actions creates an environment where children can express their joy in the Lord while building lasting memories of celebrating Christ’s birth.

Scripture

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

“Let them praise his name with dancing and make music to him with timbrel and harp.” - Psalm 149:3

Worship Ideas

  1. “Joy to the World” with Rising Actions
  • “Start crouched down low”
  • “Slowly rise with arms lifting on “Joy to the World”
  • “Spin in circles for “let earth receive her King”
  • “Make a crown with hands for “let every heart prepare Him room”
  1. “Away in a Manger” with Gentle Motions
  • “Rock baby Jesus (arms cradled)”
  • “Point up for “the stars in the bright sky”
  • “Fold hands for “little Lord Jesus asleep on the hay”
  1. “Silent Night” with Candlelight Actions
  • “Hold up “candles” (fingers pointed up)”
  • “Sway gently side to side”
  • “Make twinkling star movements”

Devotional Thought

Just as the shepherds and wise men came to worship baby Jesus, we can show our love for Him through our songs and movements. When we worship with our whole bodies, we’re telling Jesus that we love Him with all our heart. Every motion we make can be a gift to Jesus, just like the presents the wise men brought.

Dear Jesus, thank you for coming as a baby. Help us worship you with joy in our hearts and praise in our actions. Amen.

FAQs

What age group is best for motion-based Christmas songs? Motion songs work well for children ages 2-10, but can be adapted for any age. Younger children (2-5) do best with simple, repetitive motions, while older kids can handle more complex movements and longer songs.

How can I teach motion songs to a group of children? Start by demonstrating the motions slowly without music. Then add the words, and finally the music. Break down complex movements into simple steps, and remember to be enthusiastic - your energy will be contagious!

Where can I find resources for Christmas songs with motions? Many Christian children’s ministry websites offer free motion song videos and instruction sheets. Popular resources include YouTube channels dedicated to kids’ worship, children’s ministry curriculum providers, and Christian bookstores’ holiday collections.
