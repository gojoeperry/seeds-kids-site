Easter Kids Hymns: Celebrating Jesus with Joyful Songs

Introduction

Easter kids hymns play a vital role in helping children understand and celebrate the incredible story of Jesus’s resurrection. These special songs combine simple melodies with powerful truths, making the Easter message accessible and memorable for young hearts. When children learn easter kids hymns, they’re not just memorizing words - they’re building a foundation of faith through music that can last a lifetime. The repetitive nature of hymns helps reinforce biblical truths, while the melodic patterns create lasting memories that children can carry with them as they grow. By incorporating easter kids hymns into your family’s or church’s celebration, you’re giving children a meaningful way to express their faith and participate in worship during this sacred season.

Scripture

“Jesus said, ‘Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of heaven belongs to such as these.’” - Matthew 19:14

“Sing to the Lord a new song; sing to the Lord, all the earth.” - Psalm 96:1

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Is Alive” with Actions
  • “Jump up on “alive”
  • “Make cross shapes with arms”
  • “Wave hands high during chorus”
  1. “He Is Risen Indeed”
  • “Create a call-and-response pattern”
  • “One group: “He is risen!”
  • “Other group: “He is risen indeed!”
  • “Add simple hand motions”
  1. Easter Morning Praise Parade
  • “March around the room with instruments”
  • “Wave colorful streamers”
  • “Sing “Hosanna” with joy”

Devotional Thought

Jesus loves when children worship Him! Just like the children who welcomed Jesus with palm branches and songs, we can celebrate Him today. The Easter story shows us how much Jesus loves us - so much that He died for us and rose again. When we sing Easter hymns, we’re telling Jesus “thank you” and showing Him our love.

Dear Jesus, thank you for loving us so much. Help us to sing and worship you with joy. Amen.

FAQs

What age is appropriate to start teaching Easter hymns to children? Children as young as 2-3 years old can begin learning simple Easter hymns. Start with short, repetitive choruses and add hand motions to help them engage. As they grow older, introduce more complex songs and deeper meanings.

How can I make Easter hymns more engaging for kids? Add movement, instruments, and visual aids to bring the songs to life. Use pictures, hand motions, or simple props. Break down difficult words and explain their meanings. Make it interactive by taking turns leading or creating echo patterns.

Where can I find quality Easter hymns for children? Look for children’s worship resources from Christian publishers, church curriculum providers, or online platforms like YouTube Kids. Many churches also offer children’s worship albums or sheet music collections. Choose songs that are age-appropriate and doctrinally sound.
