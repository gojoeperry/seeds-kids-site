Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Is Alive” with jumping actions
  • “Jump up when singing “alive”
  • “Make cross shapes with arms”
  • “Wave hands high during the chorus”
  1. “Easter Morning Happy Day”
  • “Act out rolling the stone away”
  • “Make surprised faces like the women at the tomb”
  • “Dance and twirl during celebration parts”
  1. “God’s Love Is Like a Rainbow”
  • “Use colorful streamers or scarves”
  • “Make rainbow shapes with arms”
  • “Add simple sign language for “love”

Devotional Thought

Jesus loves children so much, and He wants them to know about His amazing resurrection story. Just like the first Easter morning brought joy to Jesus’s friends, we can celebrate that same joy today! When we sing about Easter, we’re telling the story of how much God loves us and how Jesus made a way for us to be with Him forever.

Prayer: Dear Jesus, thank you for loving us so much. Help us to sing and celebrate your resurrection with happy hearts. Amen.

FAQs

Q: What age group are these Easter worship songs best suited for? A: Most Easter songs for kids’ worship are designed for children ages 3-11, though they can be adapted for younger or older groups. The songs mentioned above work especially well for preschool and elementary-age children.

Q: How can I teach new Easter songs to children effectively? A: Start by breaking down the song into small, manageable parts. Teach the chorus first, add simple motions, and make it interactive. Remember to explain what the words mean and why we’re singing them. Practice regularly and make it fun!

Q: Where can I find quality Easter worship songs for children? A: You can find excellent Easter songs for kids through Christian children’s ministry websites, YouTube channels like Seeds Family Worship or Kids on the Move, and children’s worship curriculum providers. Many churches also offer downloadable resources for children’s worship.
