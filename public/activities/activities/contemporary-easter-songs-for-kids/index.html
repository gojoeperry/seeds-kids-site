Contemporary Easter Songs for Kids

Introduction

Contemporary Easter songs for kids provide an engaging and meaningful way for children to celebrate the resurrection of Jesus Christ. These modern worship songs help make the powerful Easter message accessible and exciting for young hearts and minds. When children connect with contemporary Easter songs for kids through familiar melodies and age-appropriate lyrics, they’re more likely to internalize the transformative story of God’s love. Music has a unique ability to embed truth deep in our hearts, making it an essential tool for teaching children about Jesus’ sacrifice and victory. Whether used in Sunday school, children’s church, or family devotions, these songs create lasting memories while building a strong spiritual foundation.

Scripture

“Jesus said to her, ‘I am the resurrection and the life. The one who believes in me will live, even though they die.’” - John 11:25

“He is not here; he has risen, just as he said.” - Matthew 28:6

Worship Ideas

  1. “Every Move I Make (Easter Version)” - Have children hop like bunnies during the chorus, then stand tall with arms raised during verses about Jesus rising.

  2. “1-2-3 Jesus Is Alive” - Create a simple hand-counting motion for numbers, then jump up shouting “Alive!” at the end of each chorus.

  3. “God’s Not Dead” - Form a circle and have kids march while singing, then freeze like statues when the music stops, celebrating that Jesus is alive.

Devotional Thought

Just as flowers bloom in spring bringing new life, Jesus rose from the dead to give us new life forever with Him. His resurrection shows us that God’s love is stronger than anything - even death! When we feel sad or afraid, we can remember that Jesus is alive and with us always.

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for loving us so much that you died and rose again. Help us remember to celebrate you not just at Easter, but every day. Amen.”

FAQs

What age group are contemporary Easter songs best suited for?

Contemporary Easter songs work well for children ages 3-12, but they’re especially effective for elementary-aged kids (5-10 years old). The songs can be adapted with simpler or more complex motions depending on the age group.

How can I teach new Easter songs to children who are shy or hesitant?

Start by teaching small portions of the song at a time, use lots of encouragement, and make it fun with simple hand motions. Consider playing the songs as background music during other activities so children become familiar with them naturally.

Where can I find quality contemporary Easter songs for kids?

Many Christian music publishers offer children’s Easter collections. Popular sources include Hillsong Kids, Orange Kids Music, and GoFish. You can also find kid-friendly Easter worship playlists on platforms like YouTube Kids and Spotify, or through children’s ministry websites.
