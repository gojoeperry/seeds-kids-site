Children’s Choir Songs for Christmas

Introduction

Teaching children’s choir songs for Christmas is one of the most delightful ways to help young ones celebrate the birth of Jesus while developing their musical gifts. These special songs create lasting memories and plant seeds of faith that can grow throughout their lives. When children learn and perform Christmas choir songs, they’re not just making beautiful music â€“ they’re sharing the gospel story through melody and rhythm. The tradition of children’s choir songs for Christmas helps young singers understand the true meaning of the season while building confidence and teamwork skills. Whether practicing at home or performing in church, these songs give children a meaningful way to participate in worship and share God’s love with others during the holiday season.

Scripture

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

“Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly, teaching and admonishing one another in all wisdom, singing psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, with thankfulness in your hearts to God.” - Colossians 3:16

Worship Ideas

  1. “Away in a Manger” with gentle rocking motions and pretend holding baby Jesus
  2. “Joy to the World” with simple hand percussion instruments (bells, triangles)
  3. “Silent Night” using sign language for key words like “holy,” “sleep,” and “peace”

Add interactive elements by having children create a living nativity scene while singing or use colorful scarves to wave during more jubilant songs. Consider incorporating simple choreography that even young children can follow.

Devotional Thought

Just as the angels sang to announce Jesus’s birth to the shepherds, we too can use our voices to tell others about God’s greatest gift. When we sing Christmas songs, we’re joining a tradition that goes back to that very first Christmas night. God loves to hear children’s voices lifted in praise, and every note we sing is a gift to Him.

Dear God, thank you for the gift of music and for sending Jesus. Help us use our voices to share Your love with others this Christmas. Amen.

FAQs

What age should children start participating in Christmas choir? Children can start participating in choir as young as age 4 or 5. At this age, they can learn simple melodies and basic motions. The key is choosing age-appropriate songs and keeping rehearsals fun and engaging.

How can I help my child learn choir songs at home? Practice songs in short, frequent sessions using recordings or videos. Make it fun by incorporating movement, using simple instruments, and praising effort. Consider creating a special practice time after dinner or before bedtime.

Where can I find good children’s Christmas choir music? Many Christian publishers offer children’s choir collections specifically for Christmas. Look for resources from publishers like LifeWay, Hal Leonard, and Alfred Music. Local Christian bookstores and online platforms like PraiseCharts also offer downloadable sheet music and practice tracks.
