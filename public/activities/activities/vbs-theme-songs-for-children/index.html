Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus’ Love is Bubbling Over” - Have children crouch down and slowly rise while making bubbling sounds, then jump up with arms raised for “bubbling over!”

  2. “God’s Love is Like a Mountain” - Create mountain shapes with arms while singing, then add stomping feet for “unmovable” and spread arms wide for “unshakeable.”

  3. “Trust and Obey” with Sign Language - Teach simple sign language motions to accompany this classic, making it interactive and memorable.

Devotional Thought

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us music and songs to praise you. Help us to sing with joy and share your love with others. Amen.”

FAQs

What age group is best suited for VBS theme songs?

VBS songs are typically designed for children ages 4-12, but can be adapted for both younger and older kids. Preschoolers enjoy simple, repetitive songs with motions, while older children can handle more complex lyrics and harmonies.

How can I teach new VBS songs effectively to children?

Start by breaking down the song into small, manageable sections. Use hand motions or visual aids to help kids remember lyrics. Practice the chorus first, then add verses gradually. Make it fun by incorporating games or challenges as they learn.

Where can I find quality VBS theme songs and resources?

Many Christian publishers offer complete VBS music packages with lyrics, motions, and backing tracks. Popular sources include LifeWay, Group Publishing, and Orange Kids. You can also find free resources on YouTube channels dedicated to children’s ministry and Christian education.
