Kids Easter Hymns

Teaching Kids Easter Hymns: A Guide for Parents and Teachers

Introduction

Teaching kids Easter hymns is a wonderful way to help children understand and celebrate the resurrection of Jesus Christ. These timeless songs not only make worship fun and engaging but also plant seeds of faith that can grow throughout their lives. When children learn kids Easter hymns, they participate in a rich tradition of praise while building their biblical foundation. The melodies and lyrics help them remember important truths about God’s love and Jesus’s sacrifice in ways that speaking alone cannot achieve. As parents and teachers, introducing kids Easter hymns during the Easter season creates meaningful memories and opportunities for spiritual conversations with our little ones.

Scripture

“Jesus said, ‘Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of heaven belongs to such as these.’” - Matthew 19:14

“Sing to the Lord a new song; sing to the Lord, all the earth.” - Psalm 96:1

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Loves Me” with Easter verses - Add simple hand motions like making a cross with arms or pointing up to heaven
  2. “God So Loved the World” - Create a circle dance where children hold hands and move in time to the music
  3. “He Is Alive” - Use rhythm instruments like tambourines or bells to celebrate the resurrection

Include colorful visual aids like picture cards showing the Easter story, and encourage children to create their own worship motions.

Devotional Thought

Jesus loves children so much that He wants them to sing and praise Him in their own special way. When we sing Easter hymns, we’re telling the amazing story of how Jesus showed His great love by dying for us and coming back to life. He wants all children to know they are precious to Him.

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for loving us so much. Help us sing happy songs about You and share Your love with others. Amen.”

FAQs

What age should children start learning Easter hymns?

Children as young as 2-3 years old can begin learning simple Easter songs. Start with short, repetitive choruses and add more complex hymns as they grow older. Even babies benefit from hearing sacred music!

How can I make Easter hymns more engaging for kids?

Make learning fun by adding hand motions, using instruments, creating action songs, or drawing pictures about the lyrics. Break down longer hymns into smaller parts and teach them gradually. Consider using modern arrangements of traditional hymns to appeal to children.

Where can I find kid-friendly Easter hymn resources?

Look for children’s worship albums, Sunday School curriculum materials, or Christian children’s websites. Many churches offer free resources, and there are numerous YouTube channels dedicated to children’s worship music. Local Christian bookstores often carry songbooks specifically designed for children’s ministry.
