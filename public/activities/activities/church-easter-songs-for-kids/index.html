Church Easter Songs For Kids

Church Easter Songs for Kids: Celebrating Jesus with Joy!

Introduction

Church Easter songs for kids provide a wonderful way to teach children about the incredible story of Jesus’s resurrection while making worship fun and memorable. When young hearts join together in singing Easter praise songs, they connect with God’s love in a powerful way that speaks their language. As parents and teachers, introducing church Easter songs for kids helps build a foundation of faith through music that can last a lifetime. These songs combine simple truths with catchy melodies, helping children understand and remember the real meaning of Easter. By incorporating movement, actions, and kid-friendly lyrics, Easter worship becomes an engaging experience that nurtures spiritual growth and creates lasting memories.

Scripture

“Jesus said, ‘Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of heaven belongs to such as these.’” - Matthew 19:14

“He is not here; he has risen, just as he said.” - Matthew 28:6

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Is Alive” - A upbeat song with jumping motions during the chorus and arm movements that mimic rolling away the stone.

  2. “Easter Morning Happy Day” - Include colorful scarves or ribbons for kids to wave while singing about Jesus’s victory.

  3. “God’s Love Is So Amazing” - Create a simple circle dance where children hold hands and move in time to the music, celebrating God’s love shown through Easter.

Devotional Thought

Just as butterflies emerge from their cocoons, Jesus came out of the tomb on Easter morning to give us new life. His love for us is so big that He chose to die and rise again so we could be His friends forever. When we sing Easter songs, we’re telling Jesus “thank you” for His amazing gift of love.

Dear Jesus, thank you for loving us so much. Help us sing with joy and remember that Easter is all about Your amazing love. Amen.

FAQs

What age group are these Easter songs best suited for?

These songs are typically best for children ages 3-11, though they can be adapted for different age groups. Younger children (3-6) respond well to simple motions and repetitive choruses, while older kids (7-11) can handle more complex lyrics and actions.

How can I teach new Easter songs to children effectively?

Start by breaking down the song into small sections, teaching one part at a time. Use hand motions or visual aids to help kids remember the words. Practice the song frequently, and always explain the meaning behind the lyrics in kid-friendly terms.

Where can I find good church Easter songs for kids?

You can find Easter songs through Christian children’s ministry websites, YouTube channels dedicated to kids’ worship, or children’s worship curriculum providers. Many churches also offer children’s worship resources, and Christian bookstores often carry kids’ worship CDs and DVDs with Easter-themed songs.
