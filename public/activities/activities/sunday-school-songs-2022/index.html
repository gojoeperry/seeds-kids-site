Scripture

“Let everything that has breath praise the Lord!” (Psalm 150:6) “Make a joyful noise to the Lord, all the earth!” (Psalm 100:1)

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus’ Love Is Bubbling Over” - Have children pretend to be bubbles, jumping up and swaying while singing about God’s overflowing love.

  2. “God’s Army” - March in place, make saluting motions, and turn in different directions while singing about being in God’s army.

  3. “Bible ABC” - Create hand motions for each letter, connecting Bible characters or stories to the alphabet through song.

Devotional Thought

Just as birds sing their beautiful songs each morning, God wants us to sing praises to Him with joy in our hearts. When we worship through song, we’re telling God how much we love Him and thank Him for all He’s done. Jesus loves to hear children’s voices lifted in praise!

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us songs to sing about your love. Help us to worship you with happy hearts. Amen.”

FAQs

What age group are these Sunday school songs best suited for? These songs are primarily designed for children ages 3-12, with simpler songs and motions for younger children and more complex lyrics for older ones. Most songs can be adapted for different age groups.

How can I teach new worship songs to children effectively? Start by breaking down the song into small sections, teaching the motions first, then adding words gradually. Use repetition, make it fun, and always explain the meaning behind the lyrics in child-friendly terms.

Where can I find new Sunday school songs and resources? You can find current Sunday school songs through Christian children’s ministry websites, YouTube channels dedicated to kids’ worship, and contemporary Christian music publishers. Many offer free resources, sheet music, and motion tutorials for teachers and parents.
