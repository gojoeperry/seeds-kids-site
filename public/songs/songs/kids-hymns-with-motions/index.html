Teaching Kids Hymns with Motions: A Joyful Way to Worship

Introduction

Teaching kids hymns with motions combines the timeless truths of Christian music with engaging physical movement, creating a powerful way for children to connect with God. When young ones learn kids hymns with motions, they’re not just memorizing words â€“ they’re experiencing worship with their whole bodies and minds. This multi-sensory approach helps children retain biblical truths while making worship fun and meaningful. Adding motions to hymns also helps fidgety kids stay focused and gives them a physical outlet for expressing their love for God. Whether you’re a parent, Sunday school teacher, or children’s ministry leader, incorporating kids hymns with motions into your teaching toolkit can transform ordinary worship times into dynamic, memorable experiences that shape young hearts for Jesus.

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Loves Me” with Heart Motions
  • “Make heart shapes with hands during “Jesus loves me”
  • “Point up for “for the Bible tells me so”
  • “Rock arms like holding a baby for “little ones to Him belong”
  1. “This Little Light of Mine”
  • “Hold up pointer finger as “light”
  • “Make hiding motion under other hand for “hide it under a bushel”
  • “Move finger in circular motion for “let it shine”
  1. “He’s Got the Whole World in His Hands”
  • “Make large circular motions for “whole world”
  • “Rock baby motion for “little bitty babies”
  • “Spreading arms wide for “you and me brother”

Devotional Thought

Just as our bodies can move and dance, our hearts can jump with joy for Jesus! When we sing and move together, we’re showing God how much we love Him with everything we have. Jesus wants us to come to Him like little children, full of joy and enthusiasm. Every motion we make can be a way to say “I love you” to God.

Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us voices to sing and bodies to move in worship to you. Help us to praise you with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength. Amen.

FAQs

What age group is best for learning hymns with motions? Children as young as 2 can begin learning simple motions, but ages 3-8 typically benefit most from this style of worship. Older children can help create new motions and lead younger ones.

How can I teach motions to a group of children effectively? Start by breaking down the song into small sections, teaching the words first, then adding motions gradually. Demonstrate clearly, practice slowly, and make it fun by encouraging participation without pressure.

Where can I find resources for kids hymns with motions? Many children’s ministry websites offer free video demonstrations, and Christian bookstores carry DVDs and songbooks specifically designed for motion-based worship. Popular channels like YouTube also feature countless tutorials for teaching kids hymns with motions.
