Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus’ Love is Bubbling Over”
  • “Cup hands together, then raise arms while “bubbling”
  • “Jump and spin when saying “bubbling over”
  • “Point to heart when mentioning love”
  1. “This Little Light of Mine”
  • “Hold up index finger as “light”
  • “Hide hands behind back for “hide it under a bushel”
  • “Make wind-blowing motion for “won’t let Satan blow it out”
  1. “Father Abraham”
  • “March in place for “right arm”
  • “Add new movements with each verse”
  • “End with whole body moving”

Devotional Thought

Just as our bodies can move and dance, God’s love moves in our hearts. When we sing and move together, we show God how happy we are to be His children. Jesus loves to see His children worship with joy and enthusiasm! Every jump, spin, and gesture can be a way to say “I love You” to God.

Dear God, thank You for giving us bodies to move and voices to sing. Help us worship You with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength. Amen.

FAQs

What age group is best for action songs with motions?

Action songs work well for children ages 2-10, but they’re especially effective for preschool and early elementary ages (3-7). Younger children naturally love to move and learn through physical activity, making this style perfect for their development stage.

How can I teach new action songs effectively?

Start by demonstrating the motions slowly while teaching one verse at a time. Use repetition and encourage participation without pressure. Make it fun by praising effort and keeping the atmosphere light and enjoyable. Consider using visual aids or props when introducing new songs.

Where can I find good action songs for kids’ worship?

Many Christian children’s ministry websites offer free resources, including YouTube channels like Seeds Family Worship or Yancy Ministries. Your church’s children’s ministry might also have songbooks or digital resources. Christian bookstores often carry DVDs and CDs specifically designed for children’s worship with motions.
