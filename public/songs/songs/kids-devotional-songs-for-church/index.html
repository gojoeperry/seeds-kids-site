Kids Devotional Songs for Church: Growing Young Hearts Through Worship

Introduction

Teaching kids devotional songs for church is one of the most powerful ways to plant seeds of faith in young hearts. These special songs help children connect with God through melody, movement, and meaningful lyrics that they can understand and remember. When children learn devotional songs early in life, they develop a foundation for lifelong worship and a personal relationship with Jesus. As parents and teachers, incorporating kids devotional songs for church into both Sunday services and daily life helps make spiritual truths stick in memorable ways. Through simple, engaging worship songs, children discover that praising God can be joyful, natural, and fun. These songs also create precious family moments and help build a Christ-centered community among young worshippers.

Scripture

“Let the little children come to me, and do not hinder them, for the kingdom of heaven belongs to such as these.” - Matthew 19:14

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Loves Me” with Sign Language Teach children to sign basic words while singing this beloved hymn. This helps engage multiple senses and makes the message more memorable.

  2. “This Little Light of Mine” with Finger Motions Have children hold up their pointer fingers as “lights” and follow motions for “hide it under a bushel - NO!” and “let it shine.”

  3. “Deep and Wide” with Full Body Movements Incorporate large arm movements to demonstrate “deep” and “wide,” making it interactive and energy-releasing.

Devotional Thought

Just as birds sing their beautiful songs to start each day, God wants us to sing praises to Him with happy hearts. When we sing devotional songs, we’re telling God how much we love Him and thanking Him for His love for us. Jesus especially loves to hear children’s voices lifted in worship!

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us voices to sing and hearts to love you. Help us make joyful music for you every day. Amen.”

FAQs

Q: What age should children start learning devotional songs? A: Children can start learning simple devotional songs as early as age 2-3. Even babies benefit from hearing worship music! Start with short, repetitive songs and gradually introduce more complex ones as they grow.

Q: How can I make devotional songs more engaging for kids? A: Add hand motions, use props like streamers or instruments, and incorporate movement. Make it interactive by letting children take turns leading motions or choosing songs. Consider using action songs during transition times or before bedtime routines.

Q: Where can I find good kids devotional songs for church? A: Many Christian publishers offer children’s worship albums and videos. Look for resources from Hillsong Kids, Seeds Family Worship, or Yancy Ministries. Your local Christian bookstore likely has collections of classic and contemporary children’s worship songs. Many free resources are also available on YouTube and Christian streaming platforms.
