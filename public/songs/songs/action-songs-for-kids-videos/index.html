Worship Ideas

  1. “Every Move I Make” - Have children follow simple hand motions that point up to heaven during “every move,” and spread arms wide during “in You.” Add jumping and spinning movements during chorus.

  2. “This Little Light of Mine” - Create hand motions for “light” (hold up pointer finger), “hide it under a bushel” (cover light with other hand), and “let it shine” (raise hands high and wiggle fingers).

  3. “Jesus Loves Me” - Incorporate sign language for “Jesus,” “loves,” and “me” while singing this classic children’s worship song.

Devotional Thought

Just as our bodies can move and dance to show joy, our hearts can also dance with happiness because of God’s love for us. When we worship with action songs, we’re using our whole selves to show God how much we love Him. Jesus welcomes children’s praise and wants us to express our love freely and joyfully.

Prayer: Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us music and movement to praise you. Help us worship you with all our heart, mind, and strength. Amen.

FAQs

Q: What age group is best suited for action songs videos? A: Action songs videos are typically most effective for children ages 2-10, with different styles appropriate for different age ranges. Toddlers and preschoolers enjoy simple, repetitive movements, while older children can handle more complex actions and lyrics.

Q: How can I incorporate action songs into our daily routine? A: Start by choosing a regular time, such as morning devotions or bedtime. Begin with one or two familiar songs, gradually adding new ones. Use action songs during transition times, car rides, or as energy breaks during the day.

Q: Where can I find quality Christian action songs videos? A: Many excellent resources are available through Christian children’s ministry channels on YouTube, such as Hillsong Kids, Seeds Family Worship, and Go Fish Kids. Christian streaming platforms and church curriculum providers also offer curated collections of action songs videos.
