Popular Christian Songs with Motions for Kids

Introduction

Teaching children to worship through popular Christian songs with motions is one of the most effective ways to help them connect with God at an early age. These engaging songs combine memorable melodies with simple physical movements, making worship both fun and meaningful for young hearts. When kids learn popular Christian songs with motions, they’re not just memorizing words – they’re expressing their love for Jesus with their whole bodies and creating lasting spiritual memories. The combination of music, movement, and biblical truth helps children internalize important faith concepts while building community with other young believers. Whether at home, church, or Christian school, these active worship experiences lay a strong foundation for lifelong faith.

Scripture

“Let everything that has breath praise the Lord!” (Psalm 150:6)

“Make a joyful noise to the Lord, all the earth! Serve the Lord with gladness! Come into his presence with singing!” (Psalm 100:1-2)

Worship Ideas

  1. “Every Move I Make” - Children sway side to side for “waves of mercy,” make wave motions for “waves of grace,” and spin in circles for “everywhere I look.”

  2. “Jesus Loves Me” with Sign Language - Teach basic sign language motions to accompany this classic, helping children connect with multiple senses.

  3. “This Little Light of Mine” - Kids hold up pointer fingers as “lights,” hide them behind their backs, and make shooing motions for “won’t let Satan blow it out.”

Devotional Thought

Just as we use our whole bodies to show excitement about things we love, God gave us our bodies to worship Him fully. When Jesus entered Jerusalem, people waved palm branches and danced with joy! Our worship movements remind us that God loves every part of us – our hearts, voices, and bodies too.

Dear God, thank you for letting us worship you with songs and movements. Help us remember that you love to see us praise you with joy! Amen.

FAQs

What age group is best for worship songs with motions? Motion songs work well for children ages 2-12, but they’re especially effective for preschool through elementary ages (3-10). Even toddlers can participate in simple movements, while older children can help lead younger ones.

How can I teach new motions effectively to a group? Break down the movements into small parts, demonstrate them slowly first, and practice without music. Then add the music gradually. Remember to keep movements simple and repetitive, and always demonstrate enthusiasm – kids will mirror your energy!

Where can I find good worship songs with motions? Many Christian children’s ministry websites offer free motion song videos, including Kids Worship Network and Go Fish Resources. Popular YouTube channels like Hillsong Kids and Seeds Family Worship also provide excellent motion-based worship content. Your local Christian bookstore likely carries DVDs specifically designed for children’s worship with motions.
