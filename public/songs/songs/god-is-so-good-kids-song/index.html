Teaching Kids “God is So Good” - A Worship Guide

Introduction

The beloved “God is so Good Kids song” is more than just a simple melody - it’s a powerful tool for teaching children about God’s endless love and faithfulness. This timeless worship song helps young hearts grasp fundamental truths about God’s character in a way they can understand and remember. When children learn the “God is so Good Kids song,” they begin to internalize the message that our Creator is loving, kind, and worthy of praise. The repetitive lyrics and easy-to-follow tune make it perfect for both home worship times and Sunday School settings. By incorporating this song into regular worship routines, parents and teachers can help build a strong foundation of faith in their children’s lives.

Scripture

“Give thanks to the Lord, for he is good; his love endures forever.” - Psalm 107:1

“Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of the heavenly lights.” - James 1:17

Worship Ideas

  1. Create simple hand motions: Raise hands up high for “God is so good,” cross arms over heart for “He’s so good to me,” point up for “God is so good”

  2. Play “Good Things Collection”: Have children gather items representing God’s goodness (flowers, family photos, favorite toys) and create a display while singing

  3. Echo Singing: Leader sings a line, kids repeat it, making it interactive and helping them learn the words

Devotional Thought

God’s goodness is like the sunshine - it’s always there, even on cloudy days. Just like parents give good things to their children, God gives us countless blessings every day. When we sing “God is so good,” we’re remembering all the ways He shows His love to us - through our families, nature, and His care for us.

Prayer: “Dear God, thank you for being so good to us. Help us to see your goodness every day and remember to thank you. Amen.”

FAQs

Q: What age group is best for learning “God is so Good”? A: This song is perfect for children ages 2-8, though even younger toddlers can clap along. Its simple lyrics and melody make it especially suitable for preschoolers beginning to learn about God.

Q: How can I make this song more engaging for active children? A: Add movement like jumping on “good,” spinning in circles for “to me,” or creating a simple dance routine. You can also use instruments like tambourines or bells to keep children engaged.

Q: Where can I find resources for teaching this song? A: Many children’s worship channels on YouTube feature this song with motions. Christian bookstores often carry CDs with kids’ worship music, and websites like Ministry-To-Children offer free teaching resources and sheet music for this classic song.
