God Is So Good Dance

Teaching Kids the God is so Good Dance

Introduction

The “God is so Good Dance” is more than just a fun movement activity - it’s a powerful way to help children express their love for God through joyful worship. When kids learn to praise God through dance and motion, they engage their whole bodies and hearts in worship. The simple yet meaningful lyrics of “God is so Good” combined with engaging movements create lasting memories and spiritual foundations. Teaching children the God is so Good Dance helps them understand worship isn’t just about sitting still - it’s about celebrating God’s love with our entire being. As parents and teachers, we can use this interactive worship experience to help kids develop a natural, enthusiastic approach to praising God that will stay with them as they grow.

Scripture

“Let them praise his name with dancing!” - Psalm 149:3

“Make a joyful noise to the Lord, all the earth! Serve the Lord with gladness! Come into his presence with singing!” - Psalm 100:1-2

Worship Ideas

  1. Basic God is so Good Dance moves:
  • “Raise hands high during “God is so good”
  • “Make heart shapes during “He’s so good to me”
  • “Spin in circles for “I praise His name”
  • “Point up for “He’s so good”
  1. Create a dance circle:
  • “Have children hold hands in a circle”
  • “Take turns letting kids suggest new moves”
  • “Add instruments like tambourines or ribbons”
  1. Action variations:
  • “Clap-and-step pattern”
  • “Sign language motions”
  • “Jump on key words”

Devotional Thought

God loves to see His children dance and celebrate His goodness! Just like a parent delights in watching their child dance with joy, our Heavenly Father smiles when we worship Him with enthusiasm. The God is so Good Dance reminds us that God’s goodness is worth celebrating every day.

Prayer: “Dear God, thank you for being so good to us. Help us remember to praise you with joy and dancing. We love you! Amen.”

FAQs

What age group is the God is so Good Dance best suited for? The dance is perfect for children ages 2-10, though it can be adapted for any age. Younger children enjoy the simple movements, while older kids can help create new variations and lead others.

How can I teach the God is so Good Dance to wiggly toddlers? Start with simple movements and demonstrate enthusiasm yourself. Break down the moves into small steps, use lots of repetition, and remember that imperfect movements are perfectly fine. The goal is joyful participation, not perfection.

Where can I find music and movement resources for the God is so Good Dance? Many children’s worship albums include “God is so Good.” You can find videos on Christian children’s YouTube channels, purchase movement tracks from Christian music publishers, or create your own simple movements to any version of the song.
