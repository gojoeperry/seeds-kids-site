Fun Children’s Choir Songs for Kids’ Worship

Introduction

Fun children’s choir songs are a powerful way to help young hearts connect with God through music and movement. When kids participate in worship through engaging choir songs, they not only learn biblical truths but also experience the joy of praising God alongside their friends. These fun children’s choir songs create lasting memories and plant seeds of faith that can grow throughout their lives. As parents and teachers, introducing children to worship music early helps them develop a natural love for praising God and understanding His word through melody. The combination of music, movement, and Scripture helps children retain biblical lessons while building confidence and social skills in a positive, Christ-centered environment.

Scripture

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” (Psalm 98:4)

“Let everything that has breath praise the Lord. Praise the Lord!” (Psalm 150:6)

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus’ Love is Bubbling Over” - Have children pretend to be bubbles floating up while singing, then “pop” on the chorus with jumping actions.

  2. “This Little Light of Mine” - Pass around battery-operated tea lights while singing, encouraging kids to lift them high during the chorus.

  3. “He’s Got the Whole World in His Hands” - Use inflatable globes or blue and green scarves while singing about God’s care for creation.

Devotional Thought

Just as birds wake up each morning singing their special songs, God gave us voices to praise Him with joy. When we sing together in choir, we’re like a beautiful garden of different flowers - each unique but creating something wonderful together. God loves hearing our voices lifted in praise, whether we’re singing quietly or loudly, on key or still learning.

Prayer: Dear God, thank you for giving us songs to sing and friends to sing with. Help us use our voices to share your love with others. Amen.

FAQs

What age should children start participating in choir?

Most children can start participating in choir activities around age 4-5, though some churches offer “praise time” for even younger toddlers. The key is finding age-appropriate songs and keeping activities engaging and movement-based.

How can I help my child practice choir songs at home?

Create a regular “worship time” during your daily routine, perhaps during car rides or before bedtime. Use hand motions, dance moves, or simple instruments to make it fun. Many songs are available on streaming platforms or YouTube for easy practice.

Where can I find quality children’s choir music resources?

Look for resources from established Christian children’s music publishers like Lifeway Kids, Group Publishing, or Seeds Family Worship. Many churches also share their children’s worship curriculum and song lists online. Local Christian bookstores often carry children’s worship CDs and songbooks.
