Christian Kids Songs With Actions

Christian Kids Songs with Actions: Making Worship Fun and Meaningful

Introduction

Christian kids songs with actions provide an engaging and memorable way for children to connect with God through music and movement. These interactive worship experiences help young hearts absorb biblical truths while creating joyful moments of praise. When children combine physical movements with spiritual songs, they engage multiple senses, making the learning experience more impactful and lasting. Christian kids songs with actions are especially effective because they transform passive listening into active participation, helping children express their faith naturally and enthusiastically. Whether at home, in Sunday school, or during children’s church, action songs create an environment where kids can worship God with their whole being - heart, mind, and body.

Scripture

“Let them praise his name with dancing and make music to him with timbrel and harp.” - Psalm 149:3

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus’ Love is Bubbling Over” - Children cup their hands together, then raise them up and outward while “bubbling,” showing how Jesus’ love flows out to others.

  2. “This Little Light of Mine” - Hold up one finger as a “light,” then perform actions for hiding under a bushel (cover light), letting it shine (wave finger high), and don’t let Satan blow it out (shake finger “no”).

  3. “Father Abraham” - This classic action song gets everyone moving with marching, arm-swinging, and leg-kicking while singing about being children of Abraham.

Devotional Thought

Just as we use our whole bodies to praise God through action songs, Jesus wants our whole hearts to love and follow Him. When we sing and move in worship, we’re showing God that we love Him with everything we have. It’s like giving God a big hug with our songs and actions!

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us songs to sing and ways to move while we praise you. Help us remember that you love watching us worship you with joy. Amen.”

FAQs

What age group is best for action songs? Action songs work well for children ages 2-12, but they’re especially effective for preschool and early elementary ages (3-8 years old). Younger children naturally learn through movement, while older kids can help lead the actions for younger ones.

How can I teach new action songs effectively? Start by teaching the actions separately from the words. Demonstrate the movements slowly, then add the words gradually. Keep it simple at first, and make it fun by praising their efforts. Remember to be enthusiastic - your energy is contagious!

Where can I find good Christian action songs for kids? You can find excellent resources on YouTube channels like Seeds Family Worship or Hillsong Kids. Christian bookstores often carry DVDs with action songs, and websites like Ministry-To-Children offer free action song resources. Many children’s ministry curriculum packages also include action songs with motions.
