Scripture Songs List for Kids Worship

Introduction

Teaching children Scripture through music is one of the most effective and joyful ways to plant God’s Word in young hearts. A thoughtfully curated scripture songs list can help kids memorize Bible verses while having fun and worshiping together. When we set God’s Word to melody, children not only learn essential truths but also develop a love for worship that can last a lifetime. Whether you’re a parent, Sunday school teacher, or children’s ministry leader, using Scripture songs creates meaningful moments of connection with both God and children. The best scripture songs list includes verses that are easy to understand and remember, set to catchy tunes that kids love to sing again and again.

Scripture

“Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly, teaching and admonishing one another in all wisdom, singing psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, with thankfulness in your hearts to God.” - Colossians 3:16

Worship Ideas

  1. “Books of the Bible Song” - Have children stand in a line, each representing one book. As the song plays, they step forward when their book is mentioned.

  2. “The Fruit of the Spirit” (Galatians 5:22-23) - Create hand motions for each fruit mentioned. For example, make a heart shape for love, smile big for joy, and fold hands for peace.

  3. “Jesus Loves Me” with Bible verses - Add Scripture references to this familiar tune, letting kids take turns leading verses.

Devotional Thought

Just as we enjoy singing our favorite songs over and over, God loves hearing our voices lifted in praise. When we sing Scripture, we’re hiding God’s Word in our hearts like precious treasure. Jesus often quoted Scripture, and when we memorize verses through song, we’re following His example.

Dear God, thank you for giving us music to help us learn Your Word. Help us to sing with joy and remember Your promises. Amen.

FAQs

What age should children start learning Scripture songs?

Children can start learning Scripture songs as early as age 2! Even toddlers can grasp simple biblical concepts through repetitive, melodic songs. As children grow, you can introduce more complex verses and concepts through age-appropriate music.

How can I make Scripture memory songs more engaging?

Add hand motions, use props, or create actions that match the words. Take breaks between verses to discuss what the words mean. Make it interactive by having children take turns leading or creating their own motions. Consider using rhythm instruments to keep the beat.

Where can I find quality Scripture songs for kids?

Many Christian children’s artists and ministries offer Scripture songs. Look for resources from Seeds Family Worship, Steve Green’s “Hide ‘Em in Your Heart” series, or Sovereign Grace Kids. Many are available on streaming platforms, YouTube, or through Christian bookstores. Your local church may also have recommended resources.
