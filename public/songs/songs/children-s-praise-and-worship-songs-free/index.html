Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Loves Me” with Sign Language Have children learn basic sign language motions while singing this classic. It helps them engage multiple senses and remember the message.

  2. “This Little Light of Mine” Action Song Kids hold up their pointer fingers as “lights” and follow simple motions: shine it around, hide it under a bush (no!), let it shine til Jesus comes.

  3. “God’s Love Is Like a Circle” with Movement Form a circle and hold hands, walking in a circle while singing about God’s never-ending love.

FAQs

What age should children start learning worship songs? Children can start learning simple worship songs as early as age 2-3. Even babies can benefit from hearing Christian music! Start with short, repetitive songs and add more complex ones as they grow.

How can I make worship time more engaging for kids? Include movement, instruments, and visual aids. Use hand motions, rhythm instruments like tambourines, or picture cards. Make it interactive by letting kids take turns leading motions or choosing songs. Keep sessions short and energetic.
