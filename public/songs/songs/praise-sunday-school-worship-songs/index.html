Teaching Kids Praise Sunday School Worship Songs

Introduction

Introducing children to praise Sunday school worship songs is one of the most joyful and important aspects of their spiritual development. These special songs help plant seeds of faith that can grow throughout their lives, creating precious memories and deep spiritual connections. When kids learn praise Sunday school worship songs, they’re not just memorizing lyrics - they’re discovering how to express their love for God in a natural, age-appropriate way. The rhythm, motions, and simple truths found in these songs make spiritual concepts tangible for young hearts. As parents and teachers, incorporating praise worship into Sunday school creates an atmosphere where children can experience God’s presence while having fun with their friends.

Scripture

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

“Let everything that has breath praise the Lord. Praise the Lord!” - Psalm 150:6

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus Loves Me” with Sign Language Teach simple hand motions to accompany each phrase, helping kids connect physical movement with worship.

  2. “This Little Light of Mine” Action Song Have children form a circle and take turns being the “light,” stepping into the middle to shine brightly.

  3. “Father Abraham” Movement Song This energetic song gets everyone moving and laughing while learning about faith and God’s promises.

Devotional Thought

God loves to hear children’s voices lifted in praise! Just like parents treasure hearing their children say “I love you,” our Heavenly Father delights in our worship. When we sing praise songs, we’re telling God how much we love Him and thanking Him for His goodness.

Dear God, thank you for giving us voices to sing and hearts to love you. Help us make joyful music for you every day. Amen.

FAQs

What age should children start learning praise Sunday school worship songs?

Children can start learning worship songs as early as age 2-3. Even toddlers can clap, dance, and begin memorizing simple phrases. As they grow, they can gradually learn more complex songs and understand deeper meanings.

How can I make worship time more engaging for kids?

Include actions, hand motions, and simple instruments like tambourines or shakers. Rotate between fast and slow songs, use visual aids like picture cards, and let children take turns leading motions. Making worship interactive keeps children engaged and excited.

Where can I find good praise Sunday school worship songs?

Many Christian publishers offer children’s worship collections, including Lifeway Kids, Hillsong Kids, and Seeds Family Worship. YouTube channels dedicated to children’s worship music are also excellent resources. Your local Christian bookstore likely has CDs and songbooks specifically designed for Sunday school use.
