Getting Started with The Singing Walrus Action Songs for Kids

Introduction

Looking for action songs for kids? The Singing Walrus download options offer an exciting way to combine movement, music, and meaningful worship. These engaging action songs for kids from The Singing Walrus download collection help children express their love for God through dynamic, full-body worship. When children engage in active worship, they’re not just moving their bodies – they’re creating lasting connections between faith and fun that can shape their spiritual journey for years to come. The combination of memorable melodies and purposeful movements helps kids internalize biblical truths while building confidence in expressing their faith. Whether you’re a parent, Sunday school teacher, or children’s ministry leader, these action-packed worship songs provide an excellent foundation for kids’ spiritual development.

Scripture

“Let them praise his name with dancing and make music to him with timbrel and harp.” - Psalm 149:3

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus’ Love Is Bubbling Over” - Have children crouch down and slowly rise while making bubbling motions, then jump up with arms raised for “bubbling over!”

  2. “God’s Love Is Like a Rainbow” - Use colorful streamers or ribbons while children move in rainbow arcs, connecting God’s promises to each color.

  3. “This Little Light of Mine” - Create hand motions for “shine,” “hide it,” and “let it shine,” encouraging children to move around the room sharing their “light.”

Devotional Thought

Just as our bodies can jump, dance, and move in amazing ways, God gave us these abilities to worship Him with our whole selves. When we use action songs, we’re telling God “thank you” with every part of us – our voices, our hands, our feet, and our hearts. Jesus loves to see His children worship with such joy and enthusiasm!

Prayer: “Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us music and movement to praise you. Help us use our whole bodies to show how much we love you. Amen.”
