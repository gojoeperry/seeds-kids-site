Kids Christian Songs with Actions: Making Worship Fun and Meaningful

Introduction

Teaching kids Christian songs with actions is one of the most effective ways to help children connect with God through worship. When young ones combine music with movement, they engage multiple senses, making the experience more memorable and meaningful. Kids Christian songs with actions not only make worship fun but also help children internalize biblical truths through repetition and physical activity. As parents and teachers, incorporating action songs into worship time creates an environment where kids can express their love for God freely and naturally. Whether at home, Sunday school, or children’s church, these dynamic worship experiences help build a strong spiritual foundation that can last a lifetime.

Scripture

“Make a joyful noise unto the Lord, all the earth: make a loud noise, and rejoice, and sing praise.” - Psalm 98:4

“Let everything that has breath praise the Lord. Praise the Lord!” - Psalm 150:6

Worship Ideas

  1. “Jesus’ Love is Bubbling Over” - Children make bubble motions with their hands while singing about Jesus’ love flowing out to others. Add jumping when the “bubbles pop”!

  2. “This Little Light of Mine” - Hold up pointer fingers as “lights,” wave them high and low, and hide them under imaginary bushels. Kids love the interactive elements and simple message.

  3. “Father Abraham” - A classic action song that gets everyone moving with marching, arm swinging, and leg kicking while celebrating being part of God’s family.

Devotional Thought

Just as our bodies move and dance when we’re happy, God loves to see us express our joy in worship. Jesus told us to become like little children in our faith, and that includes worshipping with our whole hearts and bodies. When we sing and move in praise, we’re showing God how much we love Him.

Dear God, thank you for giving us songs to sing and ways to move as we worship You. Help us to always praise You with joy and freedom. Amen.

FAQs

What age group is best for action worship songs? Action songs work well for children ages 2-12, but they’re especially effective for preschool and elementary-age kids (3-10 years old). Adjust movements and complexity based on your group’s age range.

How can I teach new action songs effectively? Break down both the words and motions into small, manageable parts. Demonstrate the actions first, then add the words gradually. Practice one section at a time before putting it all together, and always maintain enthusiasm!

Where can I find good kids Christian songs with actions? Many resources are available including YouTube channels like Kids Worship Songs, Christian children’s ministry websites, and curriculum providers like Orange and Group Publishing. Local Christian bookstores often carry DVDs and CDs with action song collections.
