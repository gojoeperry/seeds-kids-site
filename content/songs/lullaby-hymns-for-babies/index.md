## Worship Ideas
1. "Jesus Loves Me" Bedtime Version - Sing slowly and softly, adding gentle rocking motions
2. "Twinkle Twinkle, Little Star" with Christian verses - Point to stars (real or glow-in-dark)
3. "God is So Good" as a lullaby - Use soft patting motions on baby's back while singing

For older babies, add simple hand motions or finger plays that they can learn to mimic.

### Devotional Thought
Just as a loving parent holds their baby close at bedtime, our Heavenly Father holds us close always. Every lullaby we sing can remind us that God watches over us day and night. He never sleeps, never grows tired, and always keeps us safe in His care.

Prayer: "Dear God, thank you for watching over [child's name] as they sleep. Help them feel Your love and peace. Amen."

### FAQs

### What age should I start singing lullaby hymns to my baby?
You can start singing lullaby hymns from birth! Babies recognize voices and melodies even before birth, making it perfectly appropriate to begin this spiritual practice immediately. The gentle rhythms and consistent routine help establish healthy sleep patterns while creating early faith connections.

### How can I make lullaby hymns part of our bedtime routine?
Choose 2-3 favorite hymns and sing them in the same order each night. Keep the routine consistent - perhaps after bath time but before laying down. Dim the lights, hold your baby close, and sing softly. Over time, these songs will become special cues that help prepare your little one for sleep.

### Where can I find appropriate lullaby hymns for babies?
Look for Christian lullaby collections on streaming platforms like Spotify or YouTube. Many traditional hymns can be sung slowly as lullabies. Christian bookstores often carry CDs specifically designed for bedtime. Popular choices include "Jesus Loves Me," "Be Still and Know," and gentle versions of familiar worship songs.
