## Worship Ideas
1. "Jesus Loves Me" with Sign Language
Have children learn basic sign language motions while singing this classic, reinforcing the message through movement.

2. "This Little Light of Mine" Action Song
Kids hold up their "light" (pointer finger) and act out different ways to let their light shine, like hiding it under a bush (other hand) and saying "NO!"

3. "Every Move I Make" with Full-Body Motion
Incorporate jumping, spinning, and hand motions to engage children fully in worship.

### FAQs

**What age should children start learning gospel songs?**
Children can start learning gospel songs as early as age 2-3. Even babies benefit from hearing worship music! Start with simple songs like "Jesus Loves Me" and gradually introduce more complex songs as they grow.

**How can I make gospel songs more engaging for my kids?**
Add hand motions, use props like instruments or scarves, and create actions that match the lyrics. Make it interactive by letting kids suggest movements or take turns leading. Also, explain the meaning of the songs in simple terms they can understand.

**Where can I find quality kids gospel music songs?**
Look for resources from Christian children's music artists like Yancy, Seeds Family Worship, or Hillsong Kids. Many churches also offer children's worship albums, and platforms like YouTube and Spotify have dedicated playlists for kids' gospel music. Christian bookstores often carry children's worship CDs and DVDs.
