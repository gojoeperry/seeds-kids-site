## Worship Ideas
1. "Jesus Loves Me" with sign language: Teach simple hand motions for key words like "love," "Jesus," and "Bible."

2. "This Little Light of Mine" with finger actions: Use finger motions to represent the light and add movements for "let it shine."

3. "He's Got the Whole World in His Hands": Use a beach ball or soft globe to pass around while singing, helping children understand God's care for everything.

### FAQs
**Q: What age should children start learning spiritual songs?**
A: Children can start learning spiritual songs from birth! Even infants benefit from hearing gentle worship music, and by age 2-3, most children can participate actively in simple songs with motions.

**Q: How can I teach spiritual songs to wiggly preschoolers?**
A: Keep sessions short (5-10 minutes), use lots of movement and hand motions, and repeat songs frequently. Make it fun by incorporating props, instruments, or dance moves. Remember that consistency and enthusiasm are key!

**Q: Where can I find good spiritual songs for preschoolers?**
A: Look for children's worship music on Christian streaming platforms, Sunday School curriculum resources, or dedicated kids' worship channels on YouTube. Many churches also offer preschool music resources, and Christian bookstores typically have excellent children's worship collections.
