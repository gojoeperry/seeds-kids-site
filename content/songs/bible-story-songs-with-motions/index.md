## Worship Ideas
1. "Father Abraham" - Traditional motions teach about God's faithfulness while getting kids moving with arm swings, leg kicks, and turns.

2. "Deep and Wide" - Use expanding arm movements to illustrate God's vast love, adding new motions with each verse.

3. "The B-I-B-L-E" - Incorporate sign language for key words while singing about God's Word, helping children remember the importance of Scripture.

### FAQs
**Q: What age group is best for bible story songs with motions?**
A: These songs work well for children ages 2-10, but they're especially effective with preschool and early elementary children (ages 3-7) who naturally learn through movement and repetition.

**Q: How can I teach new motions to a group of children?**
A: Start by demonstrating the motions slowly without music, then add the words, and finally incorporate the melody. Break complex movements into simple steps, and praise children's efforts to build confidence.

**Q: Where can I find quality bible story songs with motions?**
A: Look for resources from Christian children's music publishers like Yancy Ministries, Seeds Family Worship, or Go Fish Kids. Many churches also share free resources on YouTube, and Christian bookstores often carry DVDs with motion-based worship songs.
