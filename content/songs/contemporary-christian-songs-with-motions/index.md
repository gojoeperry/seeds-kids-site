# Contemporary Christian Songs with Motions for Kids

### Introduction
Contemporary Christian songs with motions offer an exciting way for children to express their love for Jesus while engaging their whole bodies in worship. When kids combine music, movement, and meaningful lyrics, they create lasting connections with biblical truths that shape their growing faith. Contemporary Christian songs with motions help children remember scripture verses, biblical stories, and important lessons about God's love through kinesthetic learning.

For parents and teachers, these action-packed worship songs provide a natural outlet for children's energy while directing their enthusiasm toward praising God. The combination of modern music styles with age-appropriate movements makes worship accessible, enjoyable, and memorable for young hearts and minds.

## Worship Ideas
1. "Every Move I Make" - Children follow simple hand motions pointing up for "Na na na" and wave arms during "waves of mercy."

2. "This Little Light of Mine" - Updated version with contemporary beat. Kids hold up pointer fingers as "lights" and add movements for "hide it under a bushel - NO!"

3. "Jesus, You're My Superhero" - Children perform superhero poses while singing about Jesus' strength and love.

### Devotional Thought
Just as we use our whole bodies to show excitement about things we love, we can use our entire selves to show God how much we love Him. When we jump, dance, and move in worship, we're telling God that He makes us so happy we can't keep still! Jesus loves to see His children express their joy in creative ways.

Prayer: Dear Jesus, thank you for giving us music and movement to praise you. Help us worship you with all our heart, mind, and strength. Amen.

### FAQs

**What age group is best for contemporary Christian songs with motions?**
These songs typically work best for children ages 2-12, but can be adapted for any age. Younger children (2-5) enjoy simple, repetitive motions, while older kids (6-12) can handle more complex movements and deeper lyrics.

**How can I teach new motions to a group of children?**
Start by breaking down the movements into simple steps, demonstrate them slowly without music first, then add the music at a slower tempo. Practice one section at a time, and make it fun by encouraging children to help create new motions.

**Where can I find good contemporary Christian songs with motions?**
Look for resources from popular children's ministry channels like Hillsong Kids, Go Fish, or Yancy Ministries. Many churches also share their worship choreography on YouTube. Christian bookstores often carry DVDs specifically designed for kids' worship with motions.
