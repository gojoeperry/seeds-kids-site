## Worship Ideas
1. "Every Move I Make" - Have children form a circle and follow simple motions: jumping during the chorus, swaying during verses, and spinning when singing about God's love.

2. "This Little Light of Mine" - Use finger motions to represent the light, hide it under a bush (hands cupped), and let it shine (raise hands high). Add marching in place during the chorus.

3. "Father Abraham" - This classic action song builds excitement as movements accumulate, teaching about faith while getting energy out.

### Devotional Thought

### FAQs
Q: What age group is best suited for Christian songs with motions videos?
A: These videos are typically most effective for children ages 2-10, though they can be adapted for any age. Preschoolers particularly enjoy simple, repetitive motions, while older children can handle more complex movements and deeper lyrics.

Q: How can I incorporate these videos into our daily routine?
A: Try starting your day with a worship song during breakfast, use them as energy breaks during homeschool, or make them part of bedtime routine. Consistency helps children learn the songs and motions while building healthy spiritual habits.

Q: Where can I find quality Christian songs with motions videos?
A: You can find excellent resources on YouTube channels like Seeds Family Worship, Go Fish Kids, or Yancy Ministries. Many church curriculum providers also offer motion song videos through their digital platforms or DVDs.
