## Worship Ideas
1. "Jesus Loves Me" with Sign Language
Teach basic sign language motions while singing this classic, helping kids connect physical movement with worship.

2. "This Little Light of Mine" Action Song
Use finger motions for the "light," and encourage kids to move around during the "let it shine" parts.

3. "My God Is So Big" with Motions
Include big arm movements for "so big," muscle flexing for "so strong," and pointing upward for "there's nothing my God cannot do."

### FAQs

**Q: What age should children start learning worship songs?**
A: Children can start learning worship songs as early as age 2-3. Even babies benefit from hearing worship music! Start with simple songs and gradually introduce more complex ones as they grow.

**Q: How can I make worship time more engaging for kids?**
A: Include movement and actions, use visual aids like pictures or puppets, and choose songs with repetitive choruses. Make it interactive by letting kids take turns leading motions or suggesting songs.

**Q: Where can I find good Sunday School worship songs with lyrics?**
A: Many resources are available, including Christian children's music channels on YouTube, Sunday School curriculum websites, and children's worship DVDs. Look for well-known publishers like Hillsong Kids, Seeds Family Worship, or Go Fish.
